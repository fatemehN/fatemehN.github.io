About

I am a Machine Learning Scientist specializing in computer vision and modern AI systems. My work focuses on building reliable, high-impact models that perform in real-world conditions, not just benchmarks. I currently work as a Data Scientist at Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC).

I hold a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Université Laval and Mila, supervised by Prof. Christian Gagné and Prof. Jean-François Lalonde. My doctoral research focused on adversarial robustness of learning-based object trackers; designing attack frameworks that expose critical vulnerabilities in state-of-the-art transformer trackers.

Prior to my PhD, I conducted research on single-object tracking via deep reinforcement learning at LVSN, Université Laval, and built computer vision systems for real-world industry applications.

Representation Learning for Video Understanding
  • Framed single-object tracking as a reinforcement-learning episodic task with a Soft-Actor-Critic agent learning a motion policy from video frames.
  • Proposed the β-Multivariational Autoencoder (βMVAE) and a U-Net variant (βMVUNet) to learn an entangled Multivariate Gaussian prior (mean vector and covariance matrix) over the tracker's correlated motion parameters directly from frame patches.
  • Jointly trained the decoder to predict the target's binary segmentation mask, so a single network learns the motion distribution and the per-frame object mask used by the tracker.
